Implementation Overview

💡

Key Principle

Agile PRDs focus on individual features or epics that can be completed within 2-6 sprints, rather than attempting to document entire products upfront.

The transition from traditional to Agile PRDs requires a systematic approach that respects your team's current processes while introducing new practices that support iterative development. This guide provides a proven four-phase implementation strategy that minimizes disruption while maximizing the benefits of Agile documentation practices.

Phase 1: Team Preparation

The foundation of successful Agile PRD implementation lies in proper team preparation. This phase focuses on building understanding, assessing current practices, and establishing the collaborative framework that makes Agile PRDs effective.

Phase 1 Checklist

Questions to Consider:
  • How do we currently document product requirements?
  • Who is involved in creating and maintaining documentation?
  • How well does our current process support sprint planning?
  • What are the biggest pain points with our current approach?
  • How do we handle requirement changes during development?
Assessment Framework:
Collaboration Level

Rate 1-5: How collaborative is your current documentation process?

Agile Alignment

Rate 1-5: How well does documentation support your sprints?

Stakeholder Satisfaction

Rate 1-5: How satisfied are stakeholders with current documentation?

Maintenance Burden

Rate 1-5: How manageable is documentation maintenance?

Phase 2: Pilot Implementation

The pilot phase is where theory meets practice. Select a medium-complexity feature to test the Agile PRD approach, allowing your team to learn and adapt without the pressure of a high-stakes project.

⚠️

Pilot Selection Criteria

Choose a feature that's complex enough to test the full framework but not so critical that experimentation could impact business outcomes.

Ideal Pilot Feature Characteristics

✅
Medium Complexity

Requires 3-5 sprints to complete

✅
Cross-functional

Involves PM, design, and engineering

✅
User-facing

Has clear user value and success metrics

✅
Non-critical

Allows for experimentation and learning
